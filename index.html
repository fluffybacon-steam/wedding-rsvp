Our Story

The story began on February 28, 2020, at the Horse Inn. The bar was packed and the wait for a table was over an hour, though the time passed quickly in conversation. We were wedged between a hallway of people and a porcelain bathtub filled with cheap beer. Abby would later say she'd never had a guy ask her so many questions; for Baily it felt like someone actually cared about what he said.

Life threw us a curveball just weeks later when the world shut down. What started as "co-quarantining" quickly turned into building a life together. From buying our first home to expanding our family with pets and friends, we’ve tackled every milestone side-by-side.

Today, our "home" looks a little different. For the past three months, we have been living life on the road in our travel trailer, trekking from the northern tips of New England to the shores of Alabama. We’ve learned that wherever life takes us, we will get there together.

Frequently Asked

What should I expect?

Our exchanging of vows will be brief and informal, and want the day to be centered around spending time with our friends and family, so late arrivals or folks coming and going are welcome.

The park does require we shut down music by 10PM and the event ends by 11PM.

We will have lawn games and activities and hope folks will spend time in nature and getting to know one another!

Can I bring a plus one? Or my child?

You are welcome to bring a plus one or your children! We just ask that you RSVP with the number of folks attending in your party, so that we can ensure we have enough food.

Where should I park?

There are two parking lots in the park, and we are also allowed to have folks park in the large grassy area at the entrance to the park.

What are the amenities?

There is a historic mansion with an ADA accessible bathroom, air conditioning, and seating for around 50 folks that we will be utilizing. We will also have a large tent for shade with seating, and expect it will be cool with the breeze from the creek.
